# Remote Patient Monitoring for Heart Failure: a Structured Evidence Review

## Question

Does remote patient monitoring (RPM) of heart-failure patients after discharge reduce hospital readmissions? Secondary outcomes: mortality, adherence, cost. Criteria per the client brief (`seed/brief.md`).

## Method

- Seeded from the client's flagged list: 5 papers.  
- Search Q-01 (2026-07-04, MedSearch (fict.)): `("heart failure") AND ("remote monitoring" OR telemonitoring OR "telephone support") AND (readmission OR mortality OR adherence OR cost)` — 41 hits, 5 added.

- Screened: 12 sources → **8 included, 4 excluded** (appendix). Every decision records its criterion.

## 30-day readmissions

The primary question: short-horizon readmission effects of RPM after HF discharge.

- Telemonitored patients had a 21% relative reduction in 30-day readmission versus matched controls — Hartwell et al. (2023) [S-001]. *Multicenter cohort, n=2,140; adjusted HR 0.79 (0.68-0.92)*
- Pragmatic RCT found a significant reduction in 30-day readmission in the telemonitoring arm — Ijeoma et al. (2023) [S-006]. *n=1,022; 14.9% vs 19.6% readmitted; p=0.01*
- Meta-analysis: pooled 30-day readmission effect favors RPM, moderate heterogeneity — Farrow et al. (2024) [S-010]. *18 studies; RR 0.85 (0.77-0.94); I2=41%*
- Structured telephone support with device data showed no significant 30-day readmission effect but reduced HF-specific readmissions at 6 months — Mbeki et al. (2022) [S-002]. *RCT n=740; 30-day RR 0.93 (0.78-1.11); 6-month HF readmission RR 0.81 (0.67-0.98)*

## Mortality and long-term outcomes

Whether short-term gains persist: mortality and rehospitalization beyond 90 days.

- No mortality difference at 12 months; rehospitalization benefit attenuated after month 6 — Aldana et al. (2022) [S-007]. *Registry n=5,310; adjusted mortality HR 0.97 (0.85-1.10)*
- Six-month all-cause mortality unchanged under structured telephone support — Mbeki et al. (2022) [S-002]. *RCT n=740; 4.1% vs 4.4%*

## Adherence and engagement

Whether patients actually keep using the monitoring — the effect's delivery mechanism.

- Adherence to daily transmissions fell from 88% (month 1) to 61% (month 6) in adults over 75 — Solano et al. (2024) [S-003]. *Cohort n=612; steepest decline after care-team contact frequency dropped*
- App-based monitoring usage halved by month 9; re-engagement prompts recovered one third of lapsed users — Delgado et al. (2023) [S-011]. *Usage analysis n=3,400 accounts*

## Cost and utilization

What the program costs and saves from a payer perspective.

- Nurse-led telemonitoring produced modest net savings per patient-year from the payer perspective, driven by avoided readmissions — Okafor et al. (2024) [S-008]. *$412 net saving per patient-year (sensitivity range -$88 to $904)*

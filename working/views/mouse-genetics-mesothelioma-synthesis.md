# Synthesis — Genetic mutations and susceptibility to asbestos-induced mesothelioma in mice

_Authored synthesis over the 23 included studies (see `*-extraction.md` for the row-level data
and `*-prisma.md` for the flow). PubMed searched 2026-07-07._

## Bottom line

Across 23 in-vivo mouse studies, the dominant and highly consistent finding is that
**heterozygous loss of a tumour-suppressor gene increases susceptibility to asbestos-induced
malignant mesothelioma** — raising incidence and shortening latency versus wild-type
littermates, while rarely causing spontaneous mesothelioma without asbestos. The effect is
established for **Nf2, the Cdkn2a locus (p16^Ink4a^ and p19^Arf^), Trp53, Bap1**, and the DNA-helicase
gene **Blm**. A second, smaller group shows the opposite: knocking out selected **inflammatory
or oxidative-stress mediators** (the inflammasome adaptor Asc, mesothelial Hmgb1, the nucleotide
sanitiser Mth1) *reduces* susceptibility, implicating asbestos-driven inflammation and reactive
oxygen species as the gene–environment link. Several genes had **no measurable effect** (Nlrp3,
Mutyh, Ogg1, calretinin), and **host background genetics** modulates latency but not progression
once a tumour is established.

## Tumour-suppressor losses that increase susceptibility

- **Nf2 (merlin)** — Nf2^+/-^ mice develop mesothelioma markedly faster and more often than
  wild-type after intraperitoneal crocidolite, with biallelic Nf2 loss in the resulting tumours
  (Fleury-Feith 2003, PMID 12802287; Altomare 2005, PMID 16166281). The same Nf2^+/-^ sensitivity
  extends to a non-asbestos mineral fibre (refractory ceramic fibre; Andujar 2007, PMID 17272307).
- **Cdkn2a locus** — Heterozygosity for either product independently accelerates mesothelioma:
  p19^Arf^ (Altomare 2009, PMID 19223589) and both p16^Ink4a^ and p19^Arf^, which **cooperate** —
  Ink4a/Arf double-deficient mice develop tumours faster than either single deficiency
  (Altomare 2011, PMID 21526190).
- **Nf2 + Cdkn2a cooperation** — Compound Nf2^+/-^;Cdkn2a^+/-^ mice show accelerated onset and more
  aggressive, metastatic tumours than Nf2^+/-^ alone, via a p53–miR-34a–c-Met axis
  (Menges 2014, PMID 24371224). This double-mutant is the field's standard "accelerated" model.
- **Trp53 (p53)** — p53-deficient and p53^+/-^ mice show increased incidence and reduced latency
  after crocidolite, with loss of the wild-type allele in ~50% of tumours driving invasive growth
  (Marsella 1997, PMID 9400702; Vaslet 2002, PMID 12151629).
- **Bap1** — The most-replicated gene here. Germline Bap1^+/-^ mice have sharply higher
  asbestos-induced mesothelioma (e.g. 73% vs 32%; Xu 2014, PMID 24928783), develop it after
  **minimal** asbestos doses that rarely affect wild-type mice (Napolitano 2016, PMID 26119930;
  Kadariya 2016, PMID 26896281), and — importantly for risk debates — are also sensitised to
  low-dose **chrysotile** (serpentine), not only amphibole fibres (Kadariya 2024, PMID 38592450).
- **Blm** — Heterozygous Blm^+/-^ mice have higher incidence and shorter survival after
  intraperitoneal asbestos, extending susceptibility beyond the classic mesothelioma tumour
  suppressors to a genome-stability gene (Bononi 2020, PMID 33318203).

## An oncogene transgene as a sensitising model

- **SV40 large T antigen (MexTAg)** — Mesothelial TAg expression is not a spontaneous driver but a
  potent **co-carcinogen** with asbestos: incidence rises to ~100% (vs ~30% in wild-type), faster
  and dose-dependently on transgene copy number (Robinson 2006, PMID 17108115; Robinson 2011,
  PMID 20850297). It is widely used as a rapid, uniform asbestos-mesothelioma platform.

## Mutations that *decrease* susceptibility — the inflammation/ROS axis

- **Asc (Pycard)** — Loss of this inflammasome adaptor significantly delays onset and reduces
  incidence, linking IL-1β/IL-1R signalling to mesothelioma formation (Kadariya 2016, PMID 26935421).
- **Hmgb1** — Conditional knockout in **mesothelial** cells (the main early HMGB1 source) reduces
  and delays mesothelioma and improves survival, shifting macrophages toward M1 (Suarez 2023,
  PMID 37729199).
- **Mth1 (Nudt1)** — Knockout *lowers* crocidolite-induced mesothelioma (14.8% vs 31.7%) and
  prolongs survival in females, consistent with oxidative nucleotide damage driving carcinogenesis
  (Funahashi 2020, PMID 32183600).

## Mutations with no measurable effect

- **Nlrp3** — Dispensable for asbestos mesothelioma despite mediating the *acute* inflammatory
  response — a pointed contrast with Asc (Chow 2012, PMID 23010873).
- **Mutyh, Ogg1** — Neither base-excision-repair knockout significantly changed incidence
  (Funahashi 2020, PMID 32183600).
- **DMT1/Slc11a2** overexpression only marginally (non-significantly) delayed disease
  (Funahashi 2018, PMID 30309285).
- **Calretinin (Calb2)** — Knockout on an Nf2^+/-^ background did not alter survival (Blum 2018,
  PMID 29928505).

## Host background as a modifier

- **Collaborative Cross × MexTAg** — Across 72 genetic backgrounds (2,562 mice), host genetics
  significantly affects **latency/survival** (QTL on chromosomes 6, 12, X; eight candidate modifier
  genes validated in human datasets) but has little influence on **progression** once a tumour is
  established (Fisher 2024, PMID 38694815).

## Caveats

- Most studies use **intraperitoneal** crocidolite in a handful of lab lineages (notably the
  Fox Chase Nf2/Cdkn2a/Bap1 series and the Australian MexTAg series); route and fibre type are not
  uniform, so cross-study incidence numbers are not directly comparable.
- Incidence/latency figures are taken from abstracts where full-text values were not extracted;
  the extraction table flags where numbers were reported versus inferred.
- "Decreased susceptibility" for inflammation/ROS genes reflects their role as **downstream
  effectors** of asbestos carcinogenesis, not tumour-suppressor loss — a different mechanistic
  category from the Nf2/Cdkn2a/Trp53/Bap1 group.

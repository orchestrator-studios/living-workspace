# Screening criteria — Fascia-directed interventions and emotional / psychological outcomes in humans

**Stage:** title-abstract

**Review question.** In humans, do interventions that explicitly target the fascial / connective-tissue system (e.g. myofascial release, fascial manipulation, connective-tissue manipulation, structural integration) change emotional states, affect regulation, or psychological outcomes (anxiety, stress, depression, trauma), as measured by validated psychological instruments or physiological affect markers?

## Inclusion criteria (ALL must plausibly hold to include)
1. Human participants (any age; healthy or clinical population)
2. Applies an intervention explicitly framed as targeting fascia / myofascial / connective tissue (not generic massage, exercise, or relaxation lacking a fascial rationale)
3. Reports at least one emotional / psychological outcome measured with a validated psychological instrument or a physiological affect marker (cortisol, HRV, skin conductance)
4. Primary study reporting original data: RCT, non-randomised trial, controlled or single-arm pre/post interventional, or experimental design
5. English-language full text available

## Exclusion reasons (controlled vocabulary — use exact strings)
- `not-human`
- `no-fascia-intervention`
- `no-validated-psych-outcome`
- `not-interventional`
- `wrong-publication-type`
- `conference-abstract-only`
- `not-english`
- `no-primary-data`
- `duplicate`

Apply the conservative rule: if a record cannot be confidently ruled out, **include** it with `low` confidence.

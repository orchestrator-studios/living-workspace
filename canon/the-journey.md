# The Journey — final state, chunks, hops

A whole piece of work is a **journey**: from the state things are in now to a **final
state** — the deliverable in hand and the conditions that must be true of it. The
[move rule](the-move-rule.md) is the discipline of a single move; this document is the
frame the moves happen in. It has three altitudes, and each one carries a different
amount of commitment — deliberately.

## Start from the end

Before anything moves, describe the final state. Not the route — the destination: what
will exist when the work is done, and what must be true of it for the work to count as
done. **Write it down as a file**, first, before any other artifact of the journey exists
(in the worked example: [`DELIVERABLE.md`](../examples/lit-review/DELIVERABLE.md), written before a
single query had been drafted).

This is the load-bearing step, for two reasons:

- Every later judgment — *is this move toward the goal or sideways?* — needs something to
  be checked against. An undescribed destination can't adjudicate anything.
- Conditions written down become **acceptance criteria**: things the finished work can be
  mechanically checked against, rather than vibes it is hoped to satisfy.

## Chunk the route — coarse on purpose

With the end described, sketch the route at high altitude:

1. I do A.
2. I do B.
3. I do C.

About three chunks is the shape this usually takes — but the number isn't the rule; the
**altitude** is. Chunk exactly as finely as you are confident of the *order*, and no
finer. A chunk names **where it ends, not how it goes**: its endpoint is a state you can
describe now; its insides are unknown — and that's not a flaw in the plan, it's the reason
the plan stops there. A route sketched to the step is a guess wearing a schedule.

## Hop the insides

A **hop** is one committed move toward a target you can see clearly from where you stand
— chosen by looking at the current state, the chunk's endpoint, and the final state, and
asking: *what single move gets me closer?* Take it, re-read reality, choose again. All
the journey's uncertainty is absorbed here, where a wrong guess costs one move, not the
route.

At every hop, [the move rule](the-move-rule.md) applies: **do it, or grow it.** If the
hop is reachable with what exists, take it. If it's blocked on a missing capability —
access, understanding, presentation — then growing that capability *is* the hop, and it
deposits a permanent file the next hop reads.

## Why this shape

Plans fail across whole journeys because prediction fails with distance; pure
improvisation wanders because it has no compass. This frame splits the commitment exactly
where the confidence splits:

| Altitude | You are… | So you… |
|---|---|---|
| Final state | certain of the destination | write it down, fully |
| Chunks | confident of the order of a few big stretches | name them, coarsely |
| Hops | ignorant of the details | commit one move at a time |

Commit as little as the uncertainty allows, and no less.

The frame also recurses: a chunk is navigated the same way it was created. Its endpoint
is already described (that's what the route sketch did); if it's big, chunk it; hop the
rest.

## The example, read through this frame

[The worked example's journey](../examples/lit-review/JOURNEY.md) is this doctrine's trace, and reads
as obvious once you hold the frame:

- **Final state first** — step 2 writes `DELIVERABLE.md`: the report's contents and its
  acceptance criteria, before any search exists.
- **Three chunks** — the spec sketches the route: a validated pool of papers → the
  learnings extracted from it → the report generated from the data. Each names an
  endpoint; none says how.
- **Hops inside** — steps 3 through 8 are chunk one, discovered one move at a time:
  queries, then results, then filters, then verdicts, then the check, then the decision —
  several of them "grow it" moves (a schema here, a tool there) taken at the exact hop
  that was blocked without them.

Nothing about that process was a recipe. It's what falls out of one described
destination, one coarse sketch, and the move rule applied hop after hop.
